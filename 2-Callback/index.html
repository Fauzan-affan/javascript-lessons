What the hell is callback?

Callback is actually a function, the difference with a function in general is the way of execution. If a function is generally executed sequentially from top to bottom, the callback is executed at a certain point, that's why it's called a callback.

Callbacks are also called high-order functions. Callbacks are actually functions, the difference with functions in general is the way they are executed. If a function is generally executed directly while a callback is executed in another function via parameters.
